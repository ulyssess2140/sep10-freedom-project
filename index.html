Overview

Criminal Law is the basic building block to a safe world where criminals are put into the punishments they deserve for doing wrong to society. Lawyers do the job of either representing, or prosecuting defendents who are suspected or accused of committing a crime. But at times it becomes stressful for a lawyer to keep all the information of the case at ready to make their statement to the jury. First we need to understand the basics of a criminal lawyer(prosecutor):

  1. Prosecutors are elected officials who are responsible for investigating, charging, and prosecuting those suspected of breaking the law. They hold the most responsibility and the most power who represent the people of the US government and backed by the power of the state.
  2. There are multiple types of prosecutors that can represent different people/places. Some are U.S attorneys, District attourneys, State attorneys, Appeals prosecutors, County attorney, and Independent counsels, each as important as the other
  3. Educational requirements meet the top line, A Bachelor's Degree, LSAT (Law School Admissions Test), needs to enter Law School, and take a Bar exam. That is a lot to take in order to become a criminal prosecutor
